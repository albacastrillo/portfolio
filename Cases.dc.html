Bloat Tracker: Understanding Digestive Triggers Through Data

Helping users track daily habits and identify patterns behind bloating through a simple, intuitive mobile experience.

[Project Overview]

Tracking the causes of bloating is often inconsistent and mentally demanding. This project focused on designing an intuitive mobile app that enables users to quickly log meals, activities and symptoms to identify patterns, using iterative prototyping and user testing to refine the solution.

[Problem Statement]

Bloating is a common digestive issue, with research indicating that nearly 18% of the population experiences it at least once per week. However, identifying its causes remains challenging, as users often rely on memory or inconsistent tracking methods. This lack of structured tracking makes it difficult to uncover patterns between daily habits and symptoms.

[Process]

[01] User Research

Conducted exploratory research to understand how users track health-related symptoms.

Identified common challenges in remembering daily habits and triggers.

Reviewed existing solutions to understand gaps in usability and functionality.

[02] Insights

Tracking is often inconsistent and mentally demanding.

Users need a low-effort logging experience.

Clear visualization is key to identifying patterns.

[03] Design Solution

Developed initial wireframes focusing on simplicity and quick input.

Designed key features: meal logging, activity tracking and symptom recording.

Created interactive prototypes in Figma.

[04] Testing & Iteration

Conducted usability testing with 8 participants performing key tasks independently.

Identified friction points through task completion time, errors, and user feedback.

Iterated on the design by simplifying flows, improving labels, and reducing steps.

[Key Learnings]

Simplicity is essential

Reducing complexity significantly improves usability in habit-tracking apps.

User testing reveals real issues

Observing users uncovered friction points not identified during design.

Iteration improves outcomes

Continuous refinement led to a more intuitive and efficient experience.
